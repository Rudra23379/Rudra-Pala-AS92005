Facts about Pollution

10 facts about pollution

1.Air pollution causes about 4,000 early deaths each year in London and around 43,000 across the UK, while globally it kills more than 4.2 million people every year.

2.The World Health Organization has classified diesel exhaust and fine particulate matter as known human carcinogens, just like tobacco smoke.

3.Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) can travel deep into the lungs and bloodstream, impacting nearly every organ in the body.

4.Children and older adults are most at risk—kids growing up in polluted areas may have permanently reduced lung capacity.

5.People in ethnic minority and low-income communities in the UK are three times more likely to breathe harmfully high levels of air pollution.

6.The UK’s pollution limits for NO₂ and PM2.5 are 2–4 times weaker than what the World Health Organization recommends.

7.A single year of air pollution exposure costs the UK economy billions through lost productivity and health care expenses.

8.Indoor air pollution—especially from burning solid fuels—puts millions at risk, particularly women and children in developing countries.

9.Air pollution harms more than humans—it damages buildings, forests, crops, and contributes to acid rain.

10.Even when invisible, nitrogen dioxide (NO₂) is harmful and sometimes appears as an orange haze over cities.
